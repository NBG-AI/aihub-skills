01 · Colors

A teal-led palette
with quiet neutrals.

The brand is built on a four-step teal scale, anchored by Deep Teal as the primary ink color and supported by neutrals for body and surface. Accent application is intentional — large blocks of color appear on covers and section openers; content pages stay mostly mono-chromatic.

Usage rules

Do

  • Use Deep Teal as the primary text color on light surfaces.
  • Reserve Cyan for accent moments — rules, highlights, callouts.
  • Keep one accent color per slide; let the page breathe.
  • Cream backgrounds for divider variants — never a competing color.

Don't

  • Introduce colors outside the palette — even for charts, stick to teal tints.
  • Use gradients between teals; use solid blocks at full saturation instead.
  • Put Bright Cyan as body text — reserve it for <6 word moments.
  • Combine more than three palette colors on a single slide.
02 · Typography

Aptos for everything,
used at three weights.

The source deck uses Aptos as the master typeface (Microsoft 365 default, pairs with Aeonik on print). Set body at 22pt with line-height 1.55, titles at light-weight with negative tracking. Greek characters render at the same weights — bilingual layouts pair English in primary color with Greek in 55% opacity.

03 · Logo

Three lockups,
one consistent application.

The logo appears in three forms across the system: full-color on light, knockout on dark, and a compact wordmark for footers and small surfaces. Maintain a minimum clear-space equal to the height of the building mark. Never crop, recolor, or rearrange the elements.

04 · Grid & spacing

A 12-column grid
with a 90px safe area.

Slides are designed at 1920×1080. A 90px margin on every edge defines the safe area; inside, a 12-column grid with 24px gutters carries text and image blocks. Vertical rhythm is established by an 8px base spacing scale.

05 · Templates · Covers

Cover slides
open the conversation.

Three cover variations cover most reporting and presenting needs. All run at 1920×1080 with the title left-anchored, supporting metadata bottom-left, and the visual element (photo, color block or typographic statement) carrying the right or full surface.

06 · Templates · Section dividers

Dividers signal
a shift in chapter.

Every section opens with a divider. The big numeral cues navigation; the label and caption set up what follows. Three variations let you adjust energy — dark with image for major chapters, dark-only for subdued moments, light-bright for energetic transitions.

07 · Templates · Standard content

Content slides
do the heavy lifting.

Three patterns cover most content needs: image-led, multi-column, and stat-led. All three share the same chrome: an eyebrow tag, a title, a short accent rule, and a body block — keeping the page hierarchy predictable across a long deck.

Content · A

Image right (½).

The standard image-led content slide. Body copy and the accent rule sit on the left over half the surface; full-bleed photography carries the right. Mirror for image-left by flipping the columns.

Three columns.
